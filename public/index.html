Service Recovery

Objectives & Strategy

The core principles and customer segments for post-service follow-up.

The Objective

  • • Confirms the customer’s experience after the visit.
  • • Verifies the original concern was resolved.
  • • Identifies any unresolved issues quickly.
  • • Recovers unsold recommendations professionally.
  • • Creates a clear next step for the customer.
  • • Strengthens retention through trust and fact-based follow-up.

Core Principle

Never lead with the sale.

Lead with:

Customer Experience Vehicle Outcome Facts Next Step

The goal is to continue advising professionally after the visit to build trust and protect the vehicle.

Customer Segmentation

Do not treat every closed RO the same. Separate customers into 3 post-visit buckets.

Bucket 1

Completed, No Unsold Work

Goal: Service experience follow-up, confirm vehicle is doing well, reinforce retention, close process.

Bucket 2

Completed, Unsold Work

Goal: Service experience follow-up plus recovery of open work through a structured, low-pressure process.

Bucket 3

Urgent/Safety Declined

Goal: Higher-priority follow-up with faster timing, stronger accountability, and manager visibility when needed.

Follow-Up Timing / Task Cadence

Day 1

Task: Phone Call

If no answer: leave voicemail and send follow-up text or email.

Goal: Confirm satisfaction, confirm repair outcome, thank customer.

If reached and no issues:

Set Status = Happy / No Issues
Then close process.

If issue exists:

Set Status = Issue Not Resolved or Experience Concern
Escalate immediately.

D1

Task: Call

If no answer: voicemail + text + email

  • Thank customer
  • Confirm vehicle is doing well
  • Ask about service experience
  • Mention only the most important open recommendation first
  • Offer help scheduling

Update Status after contact appropriately.

D3

Task: Text Follow-up

Additional call ONLY if high-priority or safety item.

Goal: Reconnect naturally and reintroduce the open item without pressure.

D7

Task: Call/Voicemail + Email

Goal: Final active follow-up for the first week.

Summarize open recommendation and offer scheduling help.

D14

Task: Text or Email

Goal: Reminder and planning follow-up for open work only.

D30

Task: Text or Email

Goal: Long-tail recovery attempt for larger recommendations or pending maintenance items only.

Manager Review Recommended If:

  • Safety concern is significant

Advisor Playbook

Guidelines for conversation structure and presenting unsold work.

The 6-Step Conversation Order

01

Appreciation

Thank them for the visit.

02

Experience Check

Ask how everything went.

03

Outcome Check

Confirm the vehicle is doing well and original concern was addressed.

04

Open Item Review

If recommendations remain, discuss them using facts.

05

Offer Help

Ask whether they would like help scheduling.

06

Document Outcome

Record exact result and next step in VinSolutions.

How to Present Unsold Work

Important: Do not dump every declined item on the customer at once. Use this priority order.

PRIORITY 1

Related

Anything connected directly to the reason they came in.

" Since this relates directly to the concern you originally came in for, I wanted to bring this back to your attention first.
PRIORITY 2

Immediate

Safety-related items or items likely to worsen or create additional damage.

" This was the most urgent item noted during your inspection because of its safety impact or the potential for more damage if it is delayed.
PRIORITY 3

Maintenance

Routine services that protect long-term reliability.

" This one was not urgent that day, but it is part of keeping the vehicle on schedule and helping prevent future issues.

Process Structure

Process Name: Post-Service Follow-Up

Statuses / Steps:

  • • Post-Service Follow-Up Open
  • • Waiting for Customer Response
  • • Customer Reached
  • • Appointment Set
  • • Happy / No Issues
  • • Declined Work – Interested
  • • Declined Work – Not Ready
  • • Issue Not Resolved
  • • Experience Concern
  • • Closed – Complete

Outcome Tags:

Happy / No Issues Declined Work - Interested Declined Work - Not Ready Appointment Set Issue Not Resolved Experience Concern No Response

CRM Notes Format

Post-Service Follow-Up Result Contact Date: Contact Type: Call / Text / Email Reached Customer: Yes / No Vehicle Performing Properly: Yes / No Customer Experience Feedback: Original Concern Resolved: Yes / No Open Recommendations Reviewed: Priority Discussed: Related / Immediate / Maintenance Customer Response: Appointment Offered: Yes / No Appointment Set: Yes / No Next Follow-Up Date: Escalation Needed: Yes / No Manager Notified: Yes / No

Automation Rules (If/Then)

Condition (IF) Action (THEN)
No unsold work Create Day 1 follow-up task only. If reached & satisfied, close process.
Unsold work exists Create cadence: Day 1 (Call), Day 3 (Text/Email), Day 7 (Call+Email), Day 14 (Reminder), Day 30 (Reminder if large).
Safety/Immediate item declined Apply High Priority tag. Day 3 becomes a Call (not text). Add manager visibility. Keep open until outcome clear.
Customer reports dissatisfaction Change status to Experience Concern. Create manager follow-up task within 24 hours.
Repair did not solve issue Change status to Issue Not Resolved. Create priority callback/appt task. Notify manager/director.
Customer wants to schedule Change status to Appointment Set. Convert to active service appointment follow-up.

Manager Review Standard

Managers should review behavior, not just appointments.

Quality Assurance Checklist

Use this to evaluate advisor notes and call recordings.

  • Was contact made on time? Check cadence adherence (Day 1, 3, 7, etc.).
  • Did the advisor begin with the service experience? Should never lead with the sale.
  • Did the advisor confirm repair outcome? Ensured original concern was actually fixed.
  • Was unsold work presented using facts? No high-pressure sales tactics.
  • Was the correct priority used? Focus on Related > Immediate > Maintenance.
  • Did the advisor ask for a next step? Offered help scheduling or set future reminder.
  • Was the CRM note complete and clear? Used the standard VinSolutions note format.
  • Were escalations handled quickly? Dissatisfaction or unresolved concerns sent to mgmt immediately.
